# Executive Summary

The review of the schedule for **July 20--24, 2026** identified several
operational issues that could negatively impact patient experience,
provider utilization, and clinic efficiency if left unresolved. The
analysis covered **59 appointments across 3 clinic locations, 6
providers, and 24 patients**.

## Key Findings

### Critical Scheduling Conflicts

-   Identified **four high-priority scheduling conflicts** requiring
    immediate correction before the schedule goes live:
    -   Two provider double-bookings.
    -   One impossible provider transition between two clinic locations
        without travel time.
    -   One provider scheduled during approved PTO, resulting in three
        appointments requiring reassignment.

### Data Integrity Issues

-   Found inconsistencies affecting scheduling accuracy, including:
    -   Duplicate patient appointments with incorrect visit numbering.
    -   A treatment plan linked before completion of the initial
        evaluation.
    -   An internal provider ID mismatch that increases the risk of
        future administrative errors.

### Provider Utilization & Continuity of Care

-   Patient care continuity was fragmented by unnecessary provider
    changes despite provider availability.
-   One provider remained completely idle on a scheduled workday while
    another assumed their patients.
-   Significant workload imbalance was observed, with one therapist
    carrying nearly twice the appointment volume of another in the same
    clinic.

### Patient Retention Risks

-   Several patients were approaching completion of their treatment
    plans or had missed appointments without any follow-up scheduled,
    creating a risk of lost continuity of care and revenue.

## Operational Recommendations

1.  Resolve all scheduling conflicts before the weekly schedule is
    published.
2.  Enforce PTO as a scheduling constraint rather than a visual
    reference only.
3.  Improve data validation to prevent duplicate appointments and
    inaccurate treatment records.
4.  Balance provider workloads while maintaining continuity of care
    whenever possible.
5.  Introduce provider travel buffers between clinic locations.
6.  Standardize coverage labeling when patients are temporarily
    reassigned.
7.  Implement a recurring patient recall process for no-shows and
    patients nearing plan completion.

## Overall Assessment

The schedule is generally well organized but requires several targeted
corrections before implementation. The most significant opportunities
lie in strengthening scheduling controls, improving provider
utilization, protecting continuity of care, and introducing proactive
patient follow-up processes. Addressing these issues would improve
operational efficiency, enhance the patient experience, reduce
scheduling errors, and create a more repeatable weekly scheduling review
process.
